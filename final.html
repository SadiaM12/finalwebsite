Depression


Depression is a mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest.
Also called major depressive disorder or clinical depression, it affects how you feel, think and behave and
can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems.(src)

An estimated 3.8% of the population experience depression, including 5% of adults (4% among men and 6% among women),
and 5.7% of adults older than 60 years. Approximately 280 million people in the world have depression (1).
Depression is about 50% more common among women than among men.

There are many resources out there that can help, if you or anyone you know is suffering from depression.

How to know if you have Depression

  • Feeling hopeless and empty inside
  • Easily irritated and prone to angry outbursts
  • Have trouble sleeping or sleeping too much
  • Difficult time connecting with others
  • Losing interest in hobbies and fun activities
  • Losing your apetite or binge-eating
  • Having feelings of worthlessness
  • Having unexplained health issues like back pain or headaches
  • Comtemplating suicide or thinking about death too much

What Can I do to treat or cure Depression?

  1. Ask for help when you need it(from family, friends, and especially professionals)
  2. Set goals for yourself- This will help you find purpose and hope for your future
  3. Eat Healthy- If your struggling with losing apetite or binge-eating as a result of your depression
  4. Exercise- Its been proven that exercising encourages the brain to rewire itself in positive ways
  5. Avoid drinking alcohol and using drugs- Substance abuse is a common problem that occurs with people who have depression and they use it as a way of coping
